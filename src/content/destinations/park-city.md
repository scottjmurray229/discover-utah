## Why Should You Visit Park City?

Park City is Utah's premier mountain town — a place where Olympic history, world-class skiing, and a thriving arts scene converge in a postcard-perfect setting. Whether you're carving fresh powder at two legendary resorts or strolling the galleries along Historic Main Street, Park City delivers an experience that's equal parts adrenaline and sophistication.

## What Makes Park City Special?

Two words: Deer Valley. Actually, two more: Park City Mountain. Having two world-class ski resorts within minutes of each other is extraordinary. Add the Sundance Film Festival every January, a Main Street lined with independent restaurants and galleries, and the legacy of the 2002 Winter Olympics, and you've got one of America's most complete mountain destinations.

## When Is the Best Time to Visit Park City?

Winter (December–March) is prime time for skiing and the festival scene. Summer transforms the mountains into a hiking and mountain biking paradise with wildflower-covered trails and significantly lower hotel prices.

### Scott's Pro Tips

**Getting There** — Fly into SLC and take the 35-minute drive up Parley's Canyon. Several shuttle services run throughout the day.

**Best Time to Visit** — January for Sundance Film Festival, February–March for the best snow. Summer for hiking and mountain biking at a fraction of winter prices.

**Getting Around** — Free city bus system covers Main Street, resorts, and neighborhoods. Car useful for exploring beyond town.

**Budget Tips** — Ski midweek for lower lift ticket prices. Book lodging in SLC and commute to save 40%. Summer rates are half of winter.

**Safety** — Altitude is 7,000 feet — hydrate and take it easy on day one. Watch for black ice on roads November–April.

**Packing** — Layers are essential. Bring goggles, sunscreen (high altitude sun is intense), and proper ski gear or plan to rent.
