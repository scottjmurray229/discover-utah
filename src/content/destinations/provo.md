## Why Should You Visit Provo?

Provo sits at the base of the Wasatch Range with some of Utah's most accessible outdoor adventures right at its doorstep. Provo Canyon alone is worth the trip — a stunning drive that leads to waterfalls, reservoirs, and Sundance Mountain Resort. And with a rapidly growing food and culture scene, there's more to do here than you might expect.

## What Makes Provo Special?

The combination of affordability and access. You get Wasatch Mountain scenery and adventures at a fraction of what you'd pay in Park City, with Utah Lake adding a completely different dimension. The Sundance connection — Robert Redford's resort is just up the canyon — gives Provo an artistic streak that surprises most visitors.

## When Is the Best Time to Visit Provo?

Summer and fall (May–October) are ideal for hiking, lake activities, and the famous fall colors in Provo Canyon. Winter is excellent for skiing at Sundance and nearby resorts.

### Scott's Pro Tips

**Getting There** — Drive from SLC or take the FrontRunner commuter train. I-15 gets you there in 45 minutes.

**Best Time to Visit** — September–October for fall colors in Provo Canyon. Summer for lake and hiking. Winter for Sundance skiing.

**Getting Around** — Car is essential. UTA buses cover some routes but won't get you to trailheads or Sundance.

**Budget Tips** — Provo is significantly cheaper than Park City. Camp at Utah Lake State Park. Sundance lift tickets are cheaper midweek.

**Safety** — Provo Canyon road can be icy in winter. Utah Lake has sudden winds — check conditions before boating.

**Packing** — Hiking boots, swimsuit for the lake in summer, layers year-round. Sun protection at 4,500+ feet elevation.
