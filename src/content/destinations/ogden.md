## Why Should You Visit Ogden?

Ogden is the unsung hero of Utah's Wasatch Front. While most visitors head to Park City or Salt Lake City, Ogden quietly offers three ski resorts with shorter lift lines, a revitalized downtown centered on Historic 25th Street, and some of the best mountain biking and hiking access in the state — all at lower prices.

## What Makes Ogden Special?

Authenticity. Ogden was a railroad town before it became a mountain town, and that blue-collar grit still shows in the best way. Historic 25th Street has been transformed into a strip of independent restaurants, breweries, and shops without feeling manufactured. The ski resorts — especially Powder Mountain with its 8,000+ acres — offer a genuine local experience.

## When Is the Best Time to Visit Ogden?

Winter (December–March) for skiing with significantly fewer crowds than Park City resorts. Summer for mountain biking, hiking, and the vibrant 25th Street scene.

### Scott's Pro Tips

**Getting There** — 40 minutes north of SLC via I-15. FrontRunner train runs from SLC to Ogden station on 25th Street.

**Best Time to Visit** — January–February for deep powder at Snowbasin and Powder Mountain. Summer for mountain biking and outdoor festivals.

**Getting Around** — Car essential for reaching ski resorts and trailheads. Downtown is very walkable.

**Budget Tips** — Lift tickets here are 20-40% cheaper than Park City. Stay downtown and commute to slopes. Free concerts on 25th Street in summer.

**Safety** — Mountain roads to Powder Mountain can be treacherous in storms. Always carry chains in winter.

**Packing** — Full winter gear for ski season. Mountain bike if you have one — Ogden's trails are legendary.
